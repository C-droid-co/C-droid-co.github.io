📚 Modules — Full Curriculum

MODULE 1: OPTIONS TRADING 101

Lesson 1 — What Is An Option? (Calls & Puts)

Options give you the right, not the obligation to buy (Call) or sell (Put) a stock at a specific price (strike) before expiration.

  • Call = Bullish — you win if stock goes up.
  • Put = Bearish — you win if stock goes down.
  • Leverage: Small moves in the stock can create large percentage moves in options (both up and down).
Example: Apple at $170 — buying a $175 Call is a bet Apple goes above $175 before expiration.

Lesson 2 — Option Contract Anatomy

  • Strike Price — the price you control.
  • Expiration Date — the deadline of the contract.
  • Premium — the price you pay to enter the option.
  • Greeks — Delta, Theta, Gamma, Vega (covered later in advanced).

Lesson 3 — Why Trade Options?

  • Leverage: Turn small moves into large % returns.
  • Defined risk: If you buy options, losses are typically limited to premium paid.
  • Flexibility: Easily play long or short without borrowing shares.

MODULE 2: GETTING STARTED TRADING

Lesson 1 — Platforms + Order Types

Recommended brokers: Webull, TradingView (for charts), Robinhood, thinkorswim. Use a broker that shows options chains, bid/ask, implied volatility, and open interest.

  • Market Order — fast execution (use carefully).
  • Limit Order — control price, avoid poor fills.
  • Stop Orders — protect positions but expect slippage in volatile markets.

Lesson 2 — Choosing Contracts

  • Pick contracts with high volume + open interest (liquidity matters).
  • Avoid ultra-short weekly "lotto" contracts when you're new.
  • Stay near the current price: ATM or 1 strike ITM/OTM depending on your edge.

Lesson 3 — Risk Management

  • NEVER risk more than 1–5% of your account per trade.
  • Losses will happen — accept them and move on.
  • No revenge trading — protect capital always.
Golden Rule: "Protect capital first. Profit later."

MODULE 3: READING THE CHART

Lesson 1 — Candlestick Basics

Green = buyers (close > open). Red = sellers (close < open). Wicks show rejection. Focus on momentum candles for entries.

Lesson 2 — Support & Resistance

Support = buyer zone / price floors. Resistance = seller zone / price ceilings. Draw zones — don't chase exact levels.

Lesson 3 — Moving Averages (EMAs)

Common EMAs: 10, 20, 50. Price above these indicates bullish bias; below indicates bearish bias. Use EMAs for dynamic support/resistance.

MODULE 4: BUILDING YOUR FIRST STRATEGY

Lesson 1 — Trend Trading Made Simple

  • Find the trend (EMAs + higher highs/lows).
  • Enter pullbacks to EMAs or support zones.
  • Sell on strength — take profits fast on new moves.
Mini-Example: Enter AAPL when price pulls to 20 EMA and volume confirms. Target short-term resistance — cut losses if price closes under 50 EMA.

Lesson 2 — Scalping for Small Accounts

Trade liquid tickers: SPY, QQQ, AAPL. Use 1–5 minute charts with VWAP and short EMAs. Quick entries/exits for 10–30% option moves.

Lesson 3 — Journaling & Review

Write every trade — entry, exit, size, reason, result. Review weekly to fix mistakes and identify patterns.

🔤 Glossary — Options Terms & Fintwit / Discord Slang

All terms included — copy these to a tooltip or popover on your site for instant clarity.

CALL → You want the stock to go UP.
PUT → You want the stock to go DOWN.
STRIKE PRICE → The price the option controls.
PREMIUM → The price you pay to buy the option.
EXPIRATION → The date the option expires.
IN THE MONEY (ITM) → Option already profitable.
OUT OF THE MONEY (OTM) → Not profitable yet; cheaper but riskier.
AT THE MONEY (ATM) → Strike closest to current price.
GREEKS → Delta, Theta, Gamma, Vega (how options react).
OPEN INTEREST → How many contracts exist; higher = more liquid.
VOLUME → Contracts traded today; higher = active.
BID / ASK → Buyers (bid) / Sellers (ask); watch spread.
BAGHOLDER → Stuck in a losing play, hoping.
CUT → Close a losing trade.
SCALP → Quick in/out trade for small profit.
RIP → Stock running hard up.
DUMP → Stock tanking fast.
FOMO → Fear of missing out (avoid it).
SWEEP → Big aggressive options buy (usually institutions).
BLOCK → Large order placed less aggressively than sweep.
LOTTO → Super risky cheap play expiring soon.
SLAP / SMACK → Aggressive buys (slap ask) / sells (hit bid).
ATH / ATL → All-time high / Low.
BTFD → "Buy the f* dip".
CHOP → Sideways, no clean trades.
REJECT → Price hit level and reversed hard.
BREAKOUT / BREAKDOWN → Price breaks support/resistance strongly.
GAPPER → Stock that gaps overnight.
FADE → Trade against a move as it loses steam.
PUMP / RUG PULL → Hype move / fake breakout.
DILUTION → Company issues more shares (bearish).
SIZING UP / DOWN → Change position size.
AVERAGING DOWN → Adding to losing trade (risky).
THIN → Low liquidity; risky fills.
WICKED OUT → Stopped out by a wick spike.
VOL EXPLOSION → Sudden big move + volume spike.
STUFFED → Big seller stops upside instantly.
NO FILLS → Couldn't get filled (fast move or illiquid).
FILLER CANDLE → Useless candle with no conviction.
TRENDING / FLAT / RANGE → Market states (trending vs choppy).
ROLLING → Moving position to later expiration.
