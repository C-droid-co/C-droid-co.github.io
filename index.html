MODULE 1: OPTIONS TRADING 101

Lesson 1 — What Is An Option? (Calls & Puts)

Options give you the right, not obligation to buy (Call) or sell (Put) a stock at a specific price (strike) before expiration.

  • Call = Bullish — win if stock goes up.
  • Put = Bearish — win if stock goes down.
  • Leverage: Small stock moves = large option % moves.
Example: Apple at $170 — buying a $175 Call bets Apple exceeds $175 by expiration.

Lesson 2 — Option Contract Anatomy

  • Strike Price — price you control.
  • Expiration Date — contract deadline.
  • Premium — cost to enter the option.
  • Greeks — Delta, Theta, Gamma, Vega (advanced).

Lesson 3 — Why Trade Options?

  • Leverage: Amplify small moves into large returns.
  • Defined Risk: Losses limited to premium (if buying).
  • Flexibility: Play long or short easily.

MODULE 2: GETTING STARTED TRADING

Lesson 1 — Platforms + Order Types

Brokers: Webull, TradingView (charts), Robinhood, thinkorswim. Look for options chains, bid/ask, IV, and open interest.

  • Market Order — fast but risky.
  • Limit Order — control price, better fills.
  • Stop Orders — protect but watch slippage.

Lesson 2 — Choosing Contracts

  • High volume + open interest for liquidity.
  • Avoid short weekly "lotto" contracts early.
  • Stay near current price: ATM or 1 strike ITM/OTM.

Lesson 3 — Risk Management

  • Risk 1–5% of account per trade.
  • Accept losses; no revenge trading.
  • Protect capital always.
Golden Rule: "Protect capital first. Profit later."

MODULE 3: READING THE CHART

Lesson 1 — Candlestick Basics

Green = buyers (close > open). Red = sellers (close < open). Wicks show rejection.

Lesson 2 — Support & Resistance

Support = buyer zone. Resistance = seller zone. Draw zones, not exact levels.

Lesson 3 — Moving Averages (EMAs)

EMAs (10, 20, 50): Price above = bullish; below = bearish. Use for dynamic support/resistance.

MODULE 4: BUILDING YOUR FIRST STRATEGY

Lesson 1 — Trend Trading Made Simple

  • Find trend (EMAs + higher highs/lows).
  • Enter pullbacks to EMAs/support.
  • Sell on strength; take profits fast.
Example: Enter AAPL on 20 EMA pullback with volume. Target resistance; cut if below 50 EMA.

Lesson 2 — Scalping for Small Accounts

Trade liquid tickers: SPY, QQQ, AAPL. Use 1–5 min charts with VWAP/EMAs for 10–30% moves.

Lesson 3 — Journaling & Review

Log every trade: entry, exit, size, reason, result. Review weekly.

🔤 Glossary — Terms & Slang

Key options terms and Fintwit/Discord slang for quick reference.

CALL → Stock goes UP.
PUT → Stock goes DOWN.
STRIKE PRICE → Price option controls.
PREMIUM → Cost to buy option.
EXPIRATION → Option deadline.
ITM → In the money (profitable).
OTM → Out of the money (not profitable yet).
ATM → At the money (near current price).
GREEKS → Delta, Theta, Gamma, Vega.
OPEN INTEREST → Contracts open (liquidity).
VOLUME → Contracts traded today.
BID/ASK → Buyers/sellers; watch spread.
BAGHOLDER → Stuck in losing trade.
SCALP → Quick in/out trade.
FOMO → Fear of missing out.
SWEEP → Big institutional buy.
LOTTO → Risky, cheap, short-term play.
WICKED OUT → Stopped by wick spike.
PUMP/RUG PULL → Hype move/fake breakout.
ROLLING → Move position to later expiry.
