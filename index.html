MODULE 1: OPTIONS TRADING 101

Lesson 1 ‚Äî What Is An Option? (Calls & Puts)

Options give you the right, not obligation to buy (Call) or sell (Put) a stock at a specific price (strike) before expiration.

  • Call = Bullish ‚Äî win if stock goes up.
  • Put = Bearish ‚Äî win if stock goes down.
  • Leverage: Small stock moves can yield large option returns.

Lesson 2 ‚Äî Option Contract Anatomy

  • Strike Price ‚Äî price you control.
  • Expiration Date ‚Äî contract deadline.
  • Premium ‚Äî cost to enter the option.
  • Greeks ‚Äî Delta, Theta, Gamma, Vega (see advanced).

Lesson 3 ‚Äî Why Trade Options?

  • Leverage: Amplify returns with small capital.
  • Defined Risk: Losses limited to premium paid (when buying).
  • Flexibility: Long or short without borrowing.

MODULE 2: GETTING STARTED TRADING

Lesson 1 ‚Äî Platforms + Order Types

Recommended brokers: Webull, TradingView (charts), Robinhood, thinkorswim. Choose brokers with options chains, bid/ask, IV, and open interest.

  • Market Order ‚Äî fast but risky execution.
  • Limit Order ‚Äî control price, avoid bad fills.
  • Stop Orders ‚Äî protect but watch for slippage.

Lesson 2 ‚Äî Choosing Contracts

  • Focus on high volume + open interest for liquidity.
  • Avoid weekly "lotto" contracts early on.
  • Stay ATM or 1 strike ITM/OTM based on edge.

Lesson 3 ‚Äî Risk Management

  • Risk 1‚Äì5% of account per trade.
  • Accept losses and move on.
  • No revenge trading ‚Äî protect capital.

MODULE 3: READING THE CHART

Lesson 1 ‚Äî Candlestick Basics

Green = buyers (close > open). Red = sellers (close < open). Wicks show rejection. Focus on momentum candles.

Lesson 2 ‚Äî Support & Resistance

Support = buyer zones. Resistance = seller zones. Draw zones, not exact levels.

Lesson 3 ‚Äî Moving Averages (EMAs)

Use 10, 20, 50 EMAs. Price above = bullish; below = bearish. EMAs act as dynamic support/resistance.

MODULE 4: BUILDING YOUR FIRST STRATEGY

Lesson 1 ‚Äî Trend Trading Made Simple

  • Identify trend (EMAs + higher highs/lows).
  • Enter on pullbacks to EMAs/support.
  • Take profits on strength.

Lesson 2 ‚Äî Scalping for Small Accounts

Trade liquid tickers: SPY, QQQ, AAPL. Use 1‚Äì5 min charts with VWAP and short EMAs. Target 10‚Äì30% option moves.

Lesson 3 ‚Äî Journaling & Review

Log every trade: entry, exit, size, reason, result. Review weekly to spot patterns and improve.

üöÄ Advanced Trading Features

Deep dive into advanced options trading concepts to elevate your skills.

Greeks Deep Dive

  • Delta: Measures option price sensitivity to stock price changes. E.g., Delta 0.6 means option moves $0.60 per $1 stock move.
  • Theta: Time decay ‚Äî option loses value as expiration nears. Higher for short-dated options.
  • Gamma: Rate of Delta change. High Gamma = Delta shifts rapidly, ideal for scalping.
  • Vega: Sensitivity to implied volatility (IV). High Vega = option price swings with IV changes.

Advanced Strategies

  • Covered Calls: Sell calls against owned stock to generate income, reducing risk.
  • Credit Spreads: Sell high-premium option, buy cheaper one to limit risk. E.g., Bull Put Spread for bullish bias.
  • Iron Condors: Neutral strategy; sell OTM call/put spreads, profit in range-bound markets.
  • Straddles/Strangles: Buy call + put at same (straddle) or different (strangle) strikes for big moves.

Implied Volatility (IV) Mastery

  • IV Crush: Option prices drop post-earnings due to IV collapse. Avoid buying before events.
  • IV Rank: Compare current IV to past year. High IV Rank = sell premium; low = buy.
  • Skew: OTM puts often pricier than calls due to crash fears. Exploit for spread trades.

Position Management

  • Rolling: Move losing/winning positions to later expirations to manage risk or lock profits.
  • Hedging: Use protective puts or spreads to limit downside in volatile markets.
  • Scaling: Gradually add to winners or reduce losers to optimize capital.

Technical Analysis

  • Volume Profile: Identify high-volume nodes as key support/resistance.
  • Fibonacci Retracements: Use 38.2%, 50%, 61.8% levels for pullback entries.
  • RSI Divergence: Spot momentum shifts when price and RSI disagree.
