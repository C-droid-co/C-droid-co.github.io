MODULE 1: OPTIONS TRADING 101

Lesson 1 ‚Äî What Is An Option? (Calls & Puts)

Options give you the right, not the obligation to buy (Call) or sell (Put) a stock at a specific price (strike) before expiration.

  • Call = Bullish ‚Äî you win if stock goes up.
  • Put = Bearish ‚Äî you win if stock goes down.
  • Leverage: Small moves in the stock can create large percentage moves in options (both up and down).
Example: Apple at $170 ‚Äî buying a $175 Call is a bet Apple goes above $175 before expiration.

Lesson 2 ‚Äî Option Contract Anatomy

  • Strike Price ‚Äî the price you control.
  • Expiration Date ‚Äî the deadline of the contract.
  • Premium ‚Äî the price you pay to enter the option.
  • Greeks ‚Äî Delta, Theta, Gamma, Vega (covered later in advanced).

Lesson 3 ‚Äî Why Trade Options?

  • Leverage: Turn small moves into large % returns.
  • Defined risk: If you buy options, losses are typically limited to premium paid.
  • Flexibility: Easily play long or short without borrowing shares.

MODULE 2: GETTING STARTED TRADING

Lesson 1 ‚Äî Platforms + Order Types

Recommended brokers: Webull, TradingView (for charts), Robinhood, thinkorswim. Use a broker that shows options chains, bid/ask, implied volatility, and open interest.

  • Market Order ‚Äî fast execution (use carefully).
  • Limit Order ‚Äî control price, avoid poor fills.
  • Stop Orders ‚Äî protect positions but expect slippage in volatile markets.

Lesson 2 ‚Äî Choosing Contracts

  • Pick contracts with high volume + open interest (liquidity matters).
  • Avoid ultra-short weekly "lotto" contracts when you're new.
  • Stay near the current price: ATM or 1 strike ITM/OTM depending on your edge.

Lesson 3 ‚Äî Risk Management

  • NEVER risk more than 1‚Äì5% of your account per trade.
  • Losses will happen ‚Äî accept them and move on.
  • No revenge trading ‚Äî protect capital always.
Golden Rule: "Protect capital first. Profit later."

MODULE 3: READING THE CHART

Lesson 1 ‚Äî Candlestick Basics

Green = buyers (close > open). Red = sellers (close < open). Wicks show rejection. Focus on momentum candles for entries.

Lesson 2 ‚Äî Support & Resistance

Support = buyer zone / price floors. Resistance = seller zone / price ceilings. Draw zones ‚Äî don't chase exact levels.

Lesson 3 ‚Äî Moving Averages (EMAs)

Common EMAs: 10, 20, 50. Price above these indicates bullish bias; below indicates bearish bias. Use EMAs for dynamic support/resistance.

MODULE 4: BUILDING YOUR FIRST STRATEGY

Lesson 1 ‚Äî Trend Trading Made Simple

  • Find the trend (EMAs + higher highs/lows).
  • Enter pullbacks to EMAs or support zones.
  • Sell on strength ‚Äî take profits fast on new moves.
Mini-Example: Enter AAPL when price pulls to 20 EMA and volume confirms. Target short-term resistance ‚Äî cut losses if price closes under 50 EMA.

Lesson 2 ‚Äî Scalping for Small Accounts

Trade liquid tickers: SPY, QQQ, AAPL. Use 1‚Äì5 minute charts with VWAP and short EMAs. Quick entries/exits for 10‚Äì30% option moves.

Lesson 3 ‚Äî Journaling & Review

Write every trade ‚Äî entry, exit, size, reason, result. Review weekly to fix mistakes and identify patterns.

üî§ Glossary ‚Äî Options Terms & Fintwit / Discord Slang

All terms included ‚Äî copy these to a tooltip or popover on your site for instant clarity.

CALL ‚Üí You want the stock to go UP.
PUT ‚Üí You want the stock to go DOWN.
STRIKE PRICE ‚Üí The price the option controls.
PREMIUM ‚Üí The price you pay to buy the option.
EXPIRATION ‚Üí The date the option expires.
IN THE MONEY (ITM) ‚Üí Option already profitable.
OUT OF THE MONEY (OTM) ‚Üí Not profitable yet; cheaper but riskier.
AT THE MONEY (ATM) ‚Üí Strike closest to current price.
GREEKS ‚Üí Delta, Theta, Gamma, Vega (how options react).
OPEN INTEREST ‚Üí How many contracts exist; higher = more liquid.
VOLUME ‚Üí Contracts traded today; higher = active.
BID / ASK ‚Üí Buyers (bid) / Sellers (ask); watch spread.
BAGHOLDER ‚Üí Stuck in a losing play, hoping.
CUT ‚Üí Close a losing trade.
SCALP ‚Üí Quick in/out trade for small profit.
RIP ‚Üí Stock running hard up.
DUMP ‚Üí Stock tanking fast.
FOMO ‚Üí Fear of missing out (avoid it).
SWEEP ‚Üí Big aggressive options buy (usually institutions).
BLOCK ‚Üí Large order placed less aggressively than sweep.
LOTTO ‚Üí Super risky cheap play expiring soon.
SLAP / SMACK ‚Üí Aggressive buys (slap ask) / sells (hit bid).
ATH / ATL ‚Üí All-time high / Low.
BTFD ‚Üí "Buy the f* dip".
CHOP ‚Üí Sideways, no clean trades.
REJECT ‚Üí Price hit level and reversed hard.
BREAKOUT / BREAKDOWN ‚Üí Price breaks support/resistance strongly.
GAPPER ‚Üí Stock that gaps overnight.
FADE ‚Üí Trade against a move as it loses steam.
PUMP / RUG PULL ‚Üí Hype move / fake breakout.
DILUTION ‚Üí Company issues more shares (bearish).
SIZING UP / DOWN ‚Üí Change position size.
AVERAGING DOWN ‚Üí Adding to losing trade (risky).
THIN ‚Üí Low liquidity; risky fills.
WICKED OUT ‚Üí Stopped out by a wick spike.
VOL EXPLOSION ‚Üí Sudden big move + volume spike.
STUFFED ‚Üí Big seller stops upside instantly.
NO FILLS ‚Üí Couldn't get filled (fast move or illiquid).
FILLER CANDLE ‚Üí Useless candle with no conviction.
TRENDING / FLAT / RANGE ‚Üí Market states (trending vs choppy).
ROLLING ‚Üí Moving position to later expiration.
